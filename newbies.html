The Newbie Munch

Many people are looking around for more information about BDSM and are striving to know a bit more about it and how to navigate entry into this scene. Many feel anxious to take a first step into that unfamiliar community.

Brussels Newbies Munch offers an informative introduction to munches and the scene in a restricted group, focussing on topics for those entering the scene. We address here specifically individuals or couples being (rather) new to the world of BDSM, eager to learn a bit more about it. However, more experienced people who would like a basic introduction because they never had one – you are also welcome!

Brussels Newbies Munch is a small-group discussion led by members of the Brussels Munch team. You can enjoy a drink while we talk you through some topics of interest, share information and chat about how things work at a munch and in the scene. At the same time, you have the opportunity to meet other newcomers and exchange thoughts with them; those interested can continue their conversations or join the Brussels Munch later on (which continues till 10 PM), but this is no obligation at all.

The multicultural nature of the Brussels environment implies that those interested speak different languages. For this reason, the Brussels Newbies Munch will be moderated in English. Our Brussels Munch Team speak French, Dutch and many other languages and are very happy to help you with any questions or queries you might have, feel free to reach out to any of us.

The Brussels Newbies Munch meets regularly on the last Tuesday of the month, downtown in Brussels at an easy accessible public venue from 6:15 PM till 7:30 PM. Please respect the starting time to get yourself a drink and hear the whole introduction; Arrivals after 6:30 will be asked to leave and join next time around as you have already missed important information.

Since the Munch is organised in a “vanilla”, public, environment, you show up in your ordinary clothing; there’s no dress code. We also behave in a way that is appropriate for a public, “vanilla” venue and refrain from any display of BDSM linked behaviour or equipment.
